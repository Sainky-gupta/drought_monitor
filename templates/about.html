About the Project

AI-Powered Agricultural Drought Monitoring System

Project Overview

Our Agricultural Drought Monitoring System is an innovative AI-powered platform that predicts drought conditions at the district level across India. By leveraging satellite data, machine learning, and advanced analytics, we provide early warning systems that help farmers, policymakers, and agricultural organizations make informed decisions.

Key Features

  • 1-2 Month Early Warning: Predict drought conditions up to 2 months in advance, giving stakeholders time to prepare and mitigate risks.
  • District-Level Precision: Get granular predictions at the district level for targeted interventions.
  • Multi-Indicator Analysis: Combines SPI (Standardized Precipitation Index), NDVI (Normalized Difference Vegetation Index), and Soil Moisture data for comprehensive assessment.
  • Interactive Visualizations: Explore drought conditions through interactive maps and heatmaps across India.
  • Real-time Monitoring: Access current and historical drought conditions with detailed metrics.
  • Policy & Decision Support: Enable data-driven decision making for agricultural planning and resource allocation.

Technology Stack

Machine Learning

XGBoost model trained on historical drought data for accurate predictions.

Satellite Data

SPI, NDVI, and Soil Moisture indices from satellite observations.

Web Framework

Flask-based web application with interactive visualizations.

GIS Integration

Leaflet.js for interactive map visualizations and heatmaps.

Methodology

Our system uses a Composite Drought Index (CDI) that combines multiple indicators:

  • SPI (Standardized Precipitation Index): Measures precipitation anomalies over different time scales (1-month and 3-month).
  • NDVI Anomaly: Tracks vegetation health and greenness compared to historical averages.
  • Soil Moisture Deficit: Monitors soil water content, critical for crop health.

These indicators are normalized and combined using machine learning algorithms to generate accurate drought predictions. The system classifies drought conditions into four categories: Normal, Mild Drought, Moderate Drought, and Severe Drought.

Impact & Applications

  • Farmers: Plan crop selection, irrigation, and resource allocation based on predicted conditions.
  • Government Agencies: Allocate resources, plan relief measures, and implement drought mitigation strategies.
  • Agricultural Organizations: Make data-driven decisions for crop insurance and support programs.
  • Researchers: Study drought patterns and climate change impacts on agriculture.

Future Enhancements

  • Integration with weather forecast APIs for real-time updates
  • Mobile application for field-level access
  • Automated alert system via SMS and email
  • Historical trend analysis and forecasting
  • Integration with crop-specific recommendations
